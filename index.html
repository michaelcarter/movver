Movver.js

Movver provides the concept of a hover on touch devices

It does this by keeping a log of where you're tapping to scroll and then figures out what you're most likely looking at on the remaining viewable portion of the screen.

In the demo on the right you can see the portion of the screen highlighted in red where Movver thinks the user's hand is covering up the display. The green highlighted tiles are areas we've asked movver to keep a calculate focus for - lighest green being most focussed. We can then attach callbacks to these when the focus remains over a certain amount for a specified period of time.

Movver has many use cases:

  • Pre-caching of content behind tappable page elements
  • Lazy loading images
  • Revealing content as it's viewed
  • Mobile analytics i.e. what are your users actually looking at?
